MOBILITY ACTIVITIES

Main activities during the project were two mobility activities - training courses for youth workers, with an aim to empower experienced and non-experienced young people in their work.

TRAINING COURSE 1

01.12.2017 - 10.12.2017. Ulcinj, Montenegro

1 st training course that was organized in a vibrant multicultural city of Ulcinj was set as
a preparatory stage for youth workers from 8 European organizations to develop their
competences regarding human rights education. Youth workers were introduced to
innovative methods in dealing with human rights education and implementation of it’s
aspects in a daily work by group of experienced trainers and councilors.
Among the methods and tools used were:
Metaphorical paper exercises/ Motivational exercises;
Film presenting the history and development of Human Rights;
Facilitated group reflection of the learning to learn process by using different methods
(learning journal, images, modeling clay, mandalas etc.);
Case Studies and experiential exercises with plenary discussions;
Presentation of country photo projects;
Learning methods based on Compass, Education Pack, Companion and other HR
manuals (Simulations, Debates, Role Plays, Guided Discussions, Critical Thinking
Development Exercises);
Facilitated group reflection of the learning to learn process;
Silent floor, Individual reflection, Brainstorming, Plenary presentation;
Simulation, debates and exercises for self-awareness and tolerance building;
Self-organized preparation of small groups for the practical experience, with mentoring
from a team member;
And many more!

TRAINING COURSE 2

09.03.2018 - 18.03.2018. Tivat, Montenegro

2 nd training course was launched to assess and evaluate the practice phase and for
development of advance competencies in Human Rights Educations. 31 youth leader
and trainers went deeper in to the process of exploration of human rights educational
methods, this time enriched by some basic knowledge gained during the first training
and some practice which they delivered in their home organizations with support of the
staff members.
Second training was organized in a coastal town of Tivat, Montenegro, which offered
positive and pleasant conditions for learning, an environment safe and perfect for
process of non-formal education implementation methods with bigger groups.
Through this phase participants went through:
An intercultural learning activity for the whole group, focused on reconnecting the group,
with the intercultural learning perspective
A set of short activities for awakening creativity and innovation, followed by small group
work on developing new methods and activities for participants’ specific realities
(mentoring throughout);
Participants presentations of their projects and ideas;
Drama warm-ups: body, voice, expression; Image theatre: Small groups developing
scenarios (mentoring throughout)
Self-organised and facilitated activities delivered on spot;
Guided workshops by experienced activists and youth workers,
Q&A-s with seasoned human rights activists trough Yugoslav wars era;
Discussions about different variants of human rights education implementations
practices in 8 European countries;
And much more!
